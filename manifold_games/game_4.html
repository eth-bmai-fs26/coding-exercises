Key Insight — The Inverse Map φ⁻¹
Why Z works
Z = 0.3 · t
⟹ t = Z / 0.3
Height Z increases monotonically along the spiral — it uniquely encodes the address t.
Why X and Y fail alone
X = cos(t)  periodic
Y = sin(t)  periodic
Every full turn brings X, Y back to the same values. Many t values share the same (X, Y).
The inverse formula
φ⁻¹(X, Y, Z)
= Z / 0.3
Only one coordinate is needed. X and Y are “redundant” for recovering t from the spiral.
