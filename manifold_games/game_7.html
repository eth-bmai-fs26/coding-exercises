Key insight: Even for a simple wavy manifold (a 1D curve in ℝ²), the encoder φ⁻¹ fragments the representation into disconnected regions. Points A,B,C get mapped to one cluster; D,E,F to another — but within each cluster, the ordering can be inverted. The two clusters operate at different scales (mismatched scales). There are empty gaps between clusters — if you try to interpolate or navigate in latent space, you land in meaningless void. The decoder φ can still reconstruct the original manifold from these fragments, but you cannot use the latent space for anything meaningful — no interpolation, no smooth traversal, no generalization in latent directions.

Why Autoencoders Fail to be Homeomorphisms

What a homeomorphism requires:
A homeomorphism is a continuous bijection with a continuous inverse. For φ⁻¹ to be a homeomorphism ℝ² → ℝ, it must be continuous, injective, surjective, and have a continuous inverse φ. Topologically, this means nearby points stay nearby, and the map preserves the structure of open sets.

Why vanilla autoencoders don't guarantee this:
The autoencoder loss is typically ‖x − φ(φ⁻¹(x))‖² — it only measures reconstruction accuracy. It places no constraint on how the encoder arranges points in latent space. Two very different arrangements of points can yield identical reconstruction loss.

Consequences:

fragmentation Latent space breaks into disconnected islands with no meaningful path between them.
inversion Local neighborhoods can be mirrored or reordered.
scale mismatch Different regions get mapped to differently-scaled latent regions.
gaps Large regions of latent space correspond to nothing — decoding them produces garbage.

The fix:
Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) and other regularized methods add constraints (e.g. KL divergence to a prior) that encourage the latent space to be smooth and connected — pushing toward a topologically coherent representation.

Key Idea
Representation space cannot be navigated

Because φ⁻¹ is not a homeomorphism, the latent space is fragmented, inverted, and gapped. Reconstruction succeeds — φ∘φ⁻¹ ≈ idX on training data — but the encoder/decoder are not true topological inverses. Interpolating in latent space, sampling new points, or navigating smoothly between representations all require a coherent latent geometry that vanilla autoencoders do not provide.
