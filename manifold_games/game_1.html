Game 1

The House Game

Key Idea
Two Numbers Describe Every House

All house images share the same anchor: the bottom-left corner is fixed. A map φ: ℝ² → ℋ sends just two real numbers, (h, w), to a complete house image. Despite the image living in ~12 M-dimensional pixel space, its intrinsic "shape complexity" is only 2.

Key Idea
φ is a Homeomorphism

φ is invertible: φ⁻¹ exists and recovers (h, w) from any house image. Both φ and φ⁻¹ are smooth: a small change in (h, w) produces a small change in the image, and a small perturbation of the image corresponds to a small shift in parameters.

Panel C Smoothness of φ

A continuous path in ℝ² maps to a continuous path through house space. Small parameter changes yield small image changes, the defining property of a homeomorphism.

Takeaway
Generate Houses by Sampling ℝ²

Because φ is smooth and invertible, producing a new house is trivial: sample (h, w) ∈ ℝ² at random and apply φ. Every point in parameter space maps to a valid, realistic house. This is the core idea behind latent-space generative models.
